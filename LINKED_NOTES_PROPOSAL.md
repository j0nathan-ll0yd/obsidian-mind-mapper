## Linking Philosophy

### Full Mesh vs. Linear vs. Hub-and-Spoke

**Full Mesh** (all notes link to all other notes in cluster):
- Use for: Small clusters (2-3 notes) where all are equally related
- Example: CARE Court (2 notes), Coffee Health (2 notes)

**Linear/Sequential** (A → B → C):
- Use for: Time-sequenced stories with clear progression
- Example: High-Speed Rail funding → construction → political opposition

**Hub-and-Spoke** (research studies = hub, opinions/mandates = spokes):
- Use for: Large clusters with central research + related coverage
- Example: RTO cluster (research studies = hub, company mandates link to hub)

**Cross-Cluster Links**:
- Some notes belong to multiple clusters (e.g., Trump + HSR links to both clusters)
- These create the knowledge graph structure
